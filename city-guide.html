Indore City Guide

Your Gateway to Central India

Welcome to the vibrant city of Indore, Madhya Pradesh, India, where tradition meets modernity, and history blends seamlessly with contemporary life. Indore is the perfect destination for your upcoming conference, offering a rich cultural heritage, delectable cuisine, and a range of attractions to explore during your stay. Indore, located at 22°34'11"N latitude and 75°54'10"E longitude, this city is not only a business hub but also a treasure trove of history, culture, and culinary delights.

Omkareshwar

One of the 12 Jyotirlingas temples of India, the Shri Omkhareshwar temple is dedicated to the Hindu God Shiva, and sits peacefully on the island of Shivapuri or Mandhata, in the Narmada River, in the Khandwa district of Madhya Pradesh.

Adi Shankaracharya Statue

The 108-foot-tall statue of Adi Guru Shankaracharya represents him as a child prodigy of 12 years. It stands on a 75-foot-tall pedestal and weighs around 100 tons. The statue is installed on a 16-foot lotus made of stone.

Rajwada (Holkar Palace)

Start your journey with a visit to Rajwada, the iconic palace of the Holkar dynasty. This architectural marvel stands as a testament to the city's royal past and showcases a blend of Maratha and Mughal design elements.

Local Attractions

  • Rajwada (Holkar Palace): Start your journey with a visit to Rajwada, the iconic palace of the Holkar dynasty. This architectural marvel stands as a testament to the city's royal past and showcases a blend of Maratha and Mughal design elements.
  • Lal Baag Palace: Built in 1886, this stunning palace reflects the architectural grandeur of the Holkar rulers. The intricate designs and exquisite craftsmanship make it a must-visit destination.
  • Kanch Mandir: A unique glass temple, Kanch Mandir is an architectural marvel dedicated to Lord Mahavira. Every surface inside is adorned with glass and mirrors, creating a mesmerizing spiritual experience.
  • Central Museum: Explore the rich history of Indore at the Central Museum, which houses artifacts from various periods, including ancient sculptures, manuscripts, and historical documents.
